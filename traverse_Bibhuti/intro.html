What is Traverse Computation?

A traverse is a series of connected lines whose lengths and directions are to be measured and the process of surveying to find such measurements is known as traversing. Generally, traversing involves the measurement of the following parameters:
1. The length of each survey line.
2. The angle between the successive survey lines or the bearings of the lines.

In survey engineering, a traverse is a set of points (stations) connected by lines, and it can be either open or closed. Closed traverses form a loop, while open traverses do not. Traverse computations involve calculating coordinates based on bearing and length between points.

Types of Traverses

Open Traverse: An open traverse starts and ends at different points. These are often used when mapping linear features such as roads or pipelines.

Closed Traverse: A closed traverse forms a polygon and starts and ends at the same point, making it ideal for property boundary surveys.

Applications of Traverse Computation

Traverse computations are crucial in various surveying and engineering applications such as:

  • Property boundary surveys
  • Topographic mapping
  • Construction layout
  • Geodetic control surveys
